Landmark Type

University Art Museum

Year Built

1909 (originally built as Alumni Memorial Hall)

2009 Reopened after expansion and renovation

Introduction

The Univeristy of Michigan Museum of Art (UMMA) is one of the largest university museums in the nation. Originally built as a memorial hall for fallen alumni in the Civil War and a place to house the university's art collections, the building has now expanded greatly and holds nearly 19,000 works of art.

In addition to its large collection of artwork, UMMA holds events and different expositions throughout the year. For example, they sometimes host events known as Student Late Nights, which include activities such as collaborative art making, silent discos, music mixing, and free food. The continuous hostings of new expositions, such as the current "Art in The Age of The Internet, 1989 to Today" also keep things fresh for visitors.
